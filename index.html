About Me

Hello there and welcome to my web page! As you may have noticed, my name is Paul!

I am a software developer based in the PNW, though that's certainly not the only thing about me that makes me who I am. I also love to play and create music, run track & field, and draw pictures. In each of these passions of mine, I am driven by the desire to learn and improve. Every block of code can be refactored to be more legible, elegant, or efficient; every race or jump is an opportunity to best your past self; and you can always become more fluent on your instrument.

I also like to think about how smaller pieces work together to become something large, sometimes greater than the sum of its parts. A bass line or horn solo can be great on its own, but when combined with the rest of the band playing along, the song is taken to a whole new level. Each stroke of a pencil is fairly unremarkable in isolation, but put enough on a piece of paper and an amazing image might appear. Similarly, I like seeing the parts of a computer program work together. Small methods and blocks of code accumulate to create something that accomplishes a pretty substantial task. Figuring out which puzzle piece to use and where it goes and then finally seeing the big picture at the end is something that I enjoy.

Anyway, feel free to peruse the stuff that I put on here! I hope you find something that you like!

Projects

Swarm Intelligence Simulation

Inspired by the ability of an ant colony to locate food and communicate to each other, as well as the effort to simulate realistic flock patterns, I chose swarm intelligence as the subject of my Senior Thesis Project at Willamette University. In this application, one to two swarms of robot "bugs" are searching an environment filled with obstacles for a finite resource. The "bugs" must navigate the maze efficiently to find their target quickly, but also must communicate with the rest of the swarm in order to quickly gather the resource, as one "bug" can only carry so much back to the hive. Many variables can be set that define the behavior of the individuals within the swarm.

Braitenberg Vehicle/Genetic Algorithm Simulation

A combination of Braitenberg Vehicles and natural selection algorithms. Some vehicles are assigned the role of predator and others prey, with sensors corresponding to their desired food source and threat. Fitness is determined by how much the vehicles "eat". Traits that benefit survival are carried on to the next generation, and traits that hinder survival are eliminated.

Music

Elementary and High School

I began playing the alto saxophone in 2005 at the age of 11. While in 7th grade I was a participant in the OMEA District 3 Honor Band for alto saxophone. 7th grade also marked the beginning of my interest in jazz, as I joined the school's jazz band, which met in the mornings before school started. Once I got to the 8th grade I made the switch to tenor saxophone.

I was a member of the symphonic band, pep band, and jazz ensemble throughout my time at Forest Grove High School. My primary instrument was still the tenor saxophone, but I played baritone sax for the pep band during basketball season my senior year (our main bari sax player also happened to be on the basketball team). I again made the OMEA District 3 Honor band sophomore, junior, and senior year for tenor sax. I played in the pit band for a few of the high school's musical theatre shows, including Grease and How To Succeed in Business Without Really Trying. My senior year I also qualified as a tenor saxophonist for the Music in May Festival held at Pacific University.

High school was also when I first began gigging outside of school. Bassist Ian Lindsay and drummer Maxwell Malizia and I formed a jazz trio, mostly playing at wine shops and restaurants in the Forest Grove area.

College

I was a member of the Willamette University Jazz Collective (directed by Dr. James Miley) all four years of college playing tenor and baritone sax, as well as a few jazz combos on tenor sax. We put on multiple concerts throughout the academic year and played at the Reno Jazz Festival twice, as well as a few times at Jimmy Mak's jazz club in Portland, OR. I also took saxophone lessons under the tutelage of Sean Flannery.

Big Mama Gayle and Her Sugar Daddies

During my junior year of high school, my band director Leonard Ostwalt came up to me one day and asked "Do you want to be in a rock band?" I obviously responded with an emphatic yes, and Mr. O put me in touch with Gayle Hayden, a local band leader who had an opening for a sax player in her group, Big Mama Gayle and Her Sugar Daddies. The band plays a variety of genres spanning Rock 'n' Roll, R&B, blues, country, latin, and funk. We've played at many different events and venues along the Willamette Valley in the time since I've joined, including the McMinnville UFO Festival, at McMenamins Grand Lodge and Hotel Oregon, and others.
